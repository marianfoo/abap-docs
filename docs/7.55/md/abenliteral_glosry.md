literal

[Unnamed data object](https://help.sap.com/doc/abapdocu_755_index_htm/7.55/en-US/abenunnamed_data_object_glosry.htm "Glossary Entry") defined by direct character-like specification of a value in the [ABAP source code](https://help.sap.com/doc/abapdocu_755_index_htm/7.55/en-US/abenabap_source_code_glosry.htm "Glossary Entry") of an [ABAP program](https://help.sap.com/doc/abapdocu_755_index_htm/7.55/en-US/abenabap_program_glosry.htm "Glossary Entry") directly (see also Literals in [ABAP CDS](https://help.sap.com/doc/abapdocu_755_index_htm/7.55/en-US/abencds_literal_v2.htm)). In the general ABAP Language, untyped [numeric literals](https://help.sap.com/doc/abapdocu_755_index_htm/7.55/en-US/abennumeric_literal_glosry.htm "Glossary Entry") and untyped [character literals](https://help.sap.com/doc/abapdocu_755_index_htm/7.55/en-US/abencharacter_literal_glosry.htm "Glossary Entry") are available. In ABAP SQL, both [typed](https://help.sap.com/doc/abapdocu_755_index_htm/7.55/en-US/abentyped_literal_glosry.htm "Glossary Entry") and [untyped](https://help.sap.com/doc/abapdocu_755_index_htm/7.55/en-US/abenuntyped_literal_glosry.htm "Glossary Entry") literals are available. A [literal operator](https://help.sap.com/doc/abapdocu_755_index_htm/7.55/en-US/abenliteral_operator_glosry.htm "Glossary Entry") enables the linking of multiple literals. See also [anonymous data object](https://help.sap.com/doc/abapdocu_755_index_htm/7.55/en-US/abenanonymous_data_object_glosry.htm "Glossary Entry") and [named data object](https://help.sap.com/doc/abapdocu_755_index_htm/7.55/en-US/abennamed_data_object_glosry.htm "Glossary Entry").
