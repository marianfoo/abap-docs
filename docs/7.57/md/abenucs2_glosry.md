UCS-2

Two byte [Unicode character representation](javascript:call_link\('abenunicode_char_represent_glosry.htm'\) "Glossary Entry"). In this representation, all characters are represented by two bytes (16 bits). In UCS-2, 65536 different characters can be encoded, that correspond to the so called Basic Multilingual Plane (BMP) of the Unicode standard. In contrast to [UTF-16](javascript:call_link\('abenutf16_glosry.htm'\) "Glossary Entry"), UCS-2 does not support characters from the [surrogate area](javascript:call_link\('abensurrogate_area_glosry.htm'\) "Glossary Entry"). The ABAP programming language supports UCS-2 in [Unicode systems](javascript:call_link\('abenunicode_system_glosry.htm'\) "Glossary Entry") and therefore no [surrogates](javascript:call_link\('abensurrogates_glosry.htm'\) "Glossary Entry").
